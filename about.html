About

Artist Statement

For as long as I can remember, stories have been at the centre of my world.

Videogames, films, books, and comics weren’t just things I enjoyed, they were places I returned to, again and again. I spent a lot of my time growing up imagining characters, building worlds, and trying to understand what made certain images and moments stay with me long after I’d experienced them.

That question never really left; Now, it sits at the core of how I approach my work.

I’m drawn to pieces that feel like they exist beyond the frame, where a character suggests a history, or an environment carries a quiet sense of something having already happened. I’m less interested in spectacle for its own sake, and more interested in atmosphere, presence, and the feeling that something is there, even if it isn’t fully explained.

Art, to me, is something that lingers. It shapes how you see things, sometimes without you even realising it. The work that matters most is the kind that stays with you, settles somewhere in your mind, and becomes part of how you experience the world.

That’s the kind of work I’m trying to create.
